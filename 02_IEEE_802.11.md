# IEEE 802.11 Standards

## Description

IEEE 802.11 is a family of wireless networking standards developed by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).

These standards define how wireless devices communicate over Wi-Fi networks, including data transmission, frequencies, security, and performance.

Every modern Wi-Fi network is based on one or more IEEE 802.11 standards.

---

# What is IEEE?

IEEE stands for:

```text
Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers
```

It is an international organization that develops technology standards for networking, telecommunications, electronics, and many other fields.

---

# What is IEEE 802.11?

IEEE 802.11 defines the rules for Wireless Local Area Networks (WLANs).

It specifies:

* Wireless communication methods
* Frequency bands
* Data rates
* Channel widths
* Security mechanisms
* Device interoperability

# IEEE 802.11 (Original)

Released in 1997.

Features:

* 2.4 GHz
* Up to 2 Mbps
* First wireless networking standard

Limitations:

* Slow speed
* Limited range
* Replaced by newer standards

---

# IEEE 802.11a

Released in 1999.

Features:

* 5 GHz frequency
* Up to 54 Mbps
* Uses OFDM

Advantages:

* Less interference
* Higher speed than 802.11b

Disadvantages:

* Shorter range
* Higher cost

---

# IEEE 802.11b

Released in 1999.

Features:

* 2.4 GHz
* Up to 11 Mbps

Advantages:

* Better range
* Lower cost

Disadvantages:

* Slower speed
* More interference

---

# IEEE 802.11g

Released in 2003.

Features:

* 2.4 GHz
* Up to 54 Mbps

Advantages:

* Good speed
* Backward compatible with 802.11b

Disadvantages:

* Still affected by interference in the 2.4 GHz band

---

# IEEE 802.11n (Wi-Fi 4)

Released in 2009.

Features:

* 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz
* Up to 600 Mbps
* Introduced MIMO technology

Advantages:

* Better speed
* Better coverage
* Improved reliability

---

# What is MIMO?

MIMO stands for:

```text
Multiple Input Multiple Output
```

It uses multiple antennas for transmitting and receiving data simultaneously.

Benefits:

* Higher speed
* Better signal quality
* Improved coverage

---

# IEEE 802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5)

Released in 2013.

Features:

* 5 GHz only
* Gigabit speeds
* MU-MIMO support
* Wider channels (80 MHz / 160 MHz)

Advantages:

* Very fast
* Better streaming performance
* Reduced interference

---

# What is MU-MIMO?

MU-MIMO stands for:

```text
Multi-User Multiple Input Multiple Output
```

It allows an Access Point to communicate with multiple devices simultaneously.

Benefits:

* Better performance
* Reduced waiting time
* Higher network efficiency

---

# IEEE 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)

Released in 2019.

Features:

* 2.4 GHz
* 5 GHz
* 6 GHz (Wi-Fi 6E)
* Higher efficiency
* OFDMA support
* Improved battery life for devices

Advantages:

* Better performance in crowded environments
* Lower latency
* Higher capacity

---

# What is OFDMA?

OFDMA stands for:

```text
Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access
```

Instead of serving one device at a time, OFDMA allows multiple devices to share the same channel efficiently.

Benefits:

* Faster communication
* Lower latency
* Better network efficiency

---

# IEEE 802.11be (Wi-Fi 7)

Latest Wi-Fi generation.

Features:

* Multi-Link Operation (MLO)
* Extremely high speeds
* Lower latency
* Improved efficiency

Designed for:

* 8K video streaming
* Virtual Reality (VR)
* Cloud gaming
* AI applications

---

# Frequency Bands

| Frequency | Characteristics                      |
| --------- | ------------------------------------ |
| 2.4 GHz   | Longer range, more interference      |
| 5 GHz     | Higher speed, shorter range          |
| 6 GHz     | Highest performance, less congestion |

---

# Channel Widths

Modern Wi-Fi standards support:

* 20 MHz
* 40 MHz
* 80 MHz
* 160 MHz

Wider channels generally provide higher speeds but may increase interference depending on the environment.

---

# Backward Compatibility

Most newer Wi-Fi standards support older devices.

Example:

A Wi-Fi 6 router can usually communicate with Wi-Fi 5 and Wi-Fi 4 devices, though the connection speed depends on the capabilities of both devices.

# Summary

* IEEE 802.11 is the foundation of Wi-Fi networking.
* Different standards improve speed, range, efficiency, and security.
* 802.11n introduced MIMO.
* 802.11ac introduced MU-MIMO.
* 802.11ax introduced OFDMA and improved efficiency.
* Wi-Fi 7 builds on these improvements with higher performance and lower latency.
* Understanding IEEE 802.11 standards is essential for networking and cybersecurity professionals.
